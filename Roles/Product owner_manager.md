5.	Expression of Interest for Product owner/manager Position (EIC Transition Project)
Role Overview
We are building an agentic data analytics platform where AI agents orchestrate data processing, machine learning, and user workflows.
We are looking for a Product Owner / Product Manager to define, prioritize, and drive the product—ensuring tight alignment between user needs, system capabilities, and engineering execution.
This is a deep-tech product role, not a generic backlog manager. You will work closely with engineers across infrastructure, ML, agent systems, and frontend.
Key Responsibilities
•	Define and own the product vision, competition landscape, and roadmap
•	Translate high-level goals into clear, actionable requirements and user stories
•	Manage and prioritize the product backlog across multiple domains:
•	Agent workflows
•	Data analytics features
•	ML capabilities
•	UI/UX improvements
•	Act as the bridge between technical and non-technical stakeholders
•	Work closely with:
•	AI Architect (agent behavior and capabilities)
•	Backend/Infrastructure Engineer
•	ML Integration Engineer
•	Frontend Engineer
•	Design and execute product research and studies to retrieve valuable insights for determining acceptance criteria for features and improve user experience 
•	Drive Agile ceremonies (sprint planning, reviews, retrospectives)
•	Ensure continuous delivery and iteration aligned with user value
Required Skills
•	Proven experience  (3+ years) as a Product Manager or Product Owner in a technical domain
•	Strong understanding of software systems and architecture (you can talk fluently with engineers)
•	Experience with Agile methodologies (Scrum/Kanban)
•	Ability to write clear, structured product requirements
•	Strong prioritization and decision-making skills
•	Experience working with cross-functional technical teams
Nice to Have
•	Experience with AI/ML products or data platforms
•	Familiarity with agent systems, LLMs, or analytics tools
•	Experience defining metrics for ML/AI performance
•	Background in data analytics or data engineering workflows
•	Experience in early-stage or ambiguous environments
What Success Looks Like
•	Clear, prioritized roadmap aligned with business and technical goals
•	Well-defined user stories that engineers can execute without ambiguity
•	Fast, consistent delivery cycles with minimal rework
•	Strong alignment across backend, ML, agent, and frontend teams
•	Product decisions grounded in user value and measurable outcomes
